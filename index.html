About him

Quaid-e-Azam Pakistan was founded by Muhammad Ali Jinnah, one of the subcontinent's most powerful leaders. He was a highly intelligent and well-respected lawyer who was born in Karachi on December 25, 1876. Quaid-e-Azam entered politics to defend Muslims' rights and put forth endless effort to ensure their equality and freedom. He was determined that Muslims should have their own country where they could live in accordance with their customs and values.He led the Pakistan Movement and was successful in creating Pakistan on August 14, 1947, thanks to his unwavering resolve, integrity, and visionary leadership. He served as Pakistan's first governor general after independence and played an important role in guiding and shaping the early stages nation through its difficult early years. The nation is still motivated and guided by Quaid-e-Azam's well-known values of discipline, unity, and faith. Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah spent his last days in poor health after devoting his life to the creation of Pakistan. Despite his illness, he continued to work for the betterment of the new nation. He passed away on 11 September 1948, leaving behind a great legacy and a country built on his vision and leadership.
